Our Inspiration: The Nui Tuong Experience

How a Vietnamese community school transformed my understanding of education

Discovering Nui Tuong

My journey into educational innovation began in the heart of Vietnam's countryside at Nui Tuong, a remarkable community founded by Le Thi Hang. What started as a simple volunteering opportunity became a life-changing experience that reshaped my understanding of what education could be.

Nestled away from urban distractions, Nui Tuong wasn't just a school—it was a living, breathing community where sustainability, education, and cultural exchange intertwined seamlessly. International volunteers and local teachers worked hand-in-hand to create a learning environment that went far beyond textbooks.

Learning Through Living

At Nui Tuong, education happened everywhere—in the organic gardens where we grew our food, the open-air kitchens where we prepared meals together, and the shared spaces where we gathered for evening discussions. The line between learning and living blurred beautifully.

I remember waking before dawn with the students to tend to the gardens, the morning mist still clinging to the rice paddies. These weren't chores but valuable lessons in responsibility, sustainability, and the satisfaction of honest work. The children took pride in contributing to our community's wellbeing, whether through farming, cooking, or maintaining our shared spaces.

Cultural Exchange & Growth

What made Nui Tuong truly special was its role as a cultural bridge. As a volunteer, I helped teach English through real conversations and practical activities, while the local community taught me about Vietnamese traditions, sustainable farming, and the true meaning of community.

Lessons happened naturally—under the shade of ancient trees, during shared meals, and while working side by side in the fields. The children learned math by measuring rice portions, science through organic farming, and global citizenship through daily interactions with people from around the world.

Bringing Nui Tuong's Spirit Home

My time at Nui Tuong showed me that the most powerful education happens when learning is woven into the fabric of daily life. It taught me that children thrive when they're active participants in their community, when they can see the direct impact of their actions, and when education addresses the whole person—mind, body, and spirit.

This experience became the foundation for our Student-Centered Self-Sufficient School. We're bringing Nui Tuong's core principles—sustainable living, community responsibility, and holistic education—to create a learning environment where students don't just prepare for life but engage with it fully, just as I witnessed in that special Vietnamese community.

"Nui Tuong is more than a place—it's a movement that proves another way of living and learning is possible."

A Day in Our School

Balancing academics, practical skills, and personal growth

7:30 - 8:30 AM

Morning Rituals

Wake up, morning exercise, and personal care. Students learn time management and self-discipline while preparing for the day ahead.

8:30 - 9:30 AM

Community Breakfast & Chores

Students work in teams to prepare and serve breakfast, followed by cleaning and organizing shared spaces. Emphasizes responsibility, teamwork, and practical life skills.

9:30 - 11:00 AM

Morning Academic Block

Focused learning sessions in core subjects (Math, Language, Science) using project-based and experiential learning methods.

  • Monday/Wednesday: Math & Sciences
  • Tuesday/Thursday: Language & Social Studies
  • Friday: Integrated Project Work
11:00 AM - 12:00 PM

Physical Activity & Gardening

Students engage in sports, yoga, or work in the school garden. This block promotes physical health, environmental stewardship, and practical agricultural skills.

12:00 - 1:30 PM

Lunch & Community Time

Students share a meal they've helped prepare, followed by relaxed social time. Emphasizes nutrition, conversation skills, and community building.

1:30 - 3:00 PM

Afternoon Learning Block

Focus on creative and practical subjects that encourage innovation and critical thinking.

  • Monday/Thursday: Arts & Music
  • Tuesday/Friday: Technology & Entrepreneurship
  • Wednesday: Environmental Studies & Sustainability
3:00 - 4:30 PM

Skill Development & Electives

Students choose from various workshops and activities based on their interests and goals. Options include coding, sustainable building, culinary arts, traditional crafts, and more.

4:30 - 6:00 PM

Evening Reflection & Community Work

Students participate in community service, complete group projects, or engage in personal reflection. This time reinforces the day's lessons and prepares students for the next day.

Why This Schedule Works

This carefully crafted daily rhythm is designed with the latest research on child development and attention span in mind. Our approach is based on the understanding that 10-year-olds typically have an attention span of 20-30 minutes for general tasks, which informs how we structure learning blocks throughout the day.

Key Research-Based Principles:

  • Optimal Learning Windows: Academic blocks are limited to 90 minutes, with varied activities within each block to maintain engagement.
  • Movement Breaks: Regular physical activity helps reset attention capacity and improves cognitive function.
  • Task Variation: Alternating between different types of activities (academic, creative, physical) prevents mental fatigue.
  • Active Learning: Hands-on, project-based learning maintains higher engagement than passive instruction.

Understanding Attention Spans

Research shows that while children can focus for 20-30 minutes on engaging tasks, their sustained attention on repetitive activities may be as short as 27-30 seconds. Our schedule is designed to work with, not against, these natural attention patterns.

By varying activities and incorporating movement, we help students maintain optimal focus and make learning more effective and enjoyable.

Cognitive Science in Action

Our approach is grounded in cognitive science principles that recognize the importance of:

  • Spaced Learning: Breaking learning into smaller, manageable chunks with breaks in between.
  • Interleaved Practice: Mixing different but related topics to enhance learning and retention.
  • Active Recall: Encouraging students to retrieve information through questions and discussions.
  • Multisensory Learning: Engaging multiple senses to reinforce learning and memory.

Personalized Attention

Recognizing that every child is unique, we implement strategies to accommodate different learning styles and attention patterns:

  • Flexible seating options to match individual comfort needs
  • Choice-based learning activities to increase engagement
  • Regular check-ins to monitor focus and adjust as needed
  • Mindfulness exercises to improve concentration

Measuring Success

We continuously assess and adapt our approach based on:

  • Student engagement levels throughout activities
  • Retention and application of learned concepts
  • Self-reported focus and enjoyment
  • Observations of natural attention patterns

"By understanding and working with children's natural attention spans, we create an environment where learning is both effective and enjoyable."

Comprehensive Sports Program

Our school emphasizes daily physical activity as a cornerstone of student development. Through a diverse sports curriculum, students will:

  • Develop physical fitness, coordination, and motor skills
  • Learn the value of teamwork, discipline, and perseverance
  • Understand the connection between physical activity and mental health
  • Participate in both competitive and recreational sports
  • Learn about sports science and kinesiology

Food Education & Culture

We believe food education is essential for lifelong health. Our program includes:

  • Hands-on cooking classes teaching nutritious meal preparation
  • School garden program connecting students to food sources
  • Nutrition education integrated across the curriculum
  • Cultural food celebrations and global cuisine exploration
  • Sustainable food practices and waste reduction

Integrated Wellness Approach

Our holistic approach combines physical activity and nutrition for complete student wellness:

  • Daily physical education and movement breaks
  • Mindfulness and stress reduction practices
  • Community meals with locally-sourced, seasonal ingredients
  • Education on the mind-body connection
  • Partnerships with local farms and food producers

Why It Matters

Research shows that students who engage in regular physical activity and understand nutrition perform better academically, have improved concentration, and develop lifelong healthy habits. Our program goes beyond traditional physical education to create a culture of wellness that supports academic achievement and personal growth.
